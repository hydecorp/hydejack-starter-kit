<td valign="top" class="mcnTextContent" style="padding: 0px 18px 9px;line-height: 150%;mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;word-break: break-word;color: var(--body-color);font-family: 'Lora', Georgia, 'Times New Roman', serif;font-size: 16px;text-align: left;">

    <p style="line-height: 150%;margin: 10px 0;padding: 0;mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: var(--body-color);font-family: 'Lora', Georgia, 'Times New Roman', serif;font-size: 16px;text-align: left;"><strong>Blue Moon and the BE-7 rocket engine.</strong> Jeff Bezos revealed last week (<a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GQ98hGUe6FM" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: var(--mc-link-color);font-weight: normal;text-decoration: underline;" target="_blank">full video</a>) that Blue Origin has been working for three years on a moon lander called <a href="https://www.blueorigin.com/blue-moon" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: var(--mc-link-color);font-weight: normal;text-decoration: underline;" target="_blank">Blue Moon</a> and a new rocket engine, <a href="https://www.blueorigin.com/engines/be-7" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: var(--mc-link-color);font-weight: normal;text-decoration: underline;" target="_blank">BE-7</a>, to power it. The stock lander is designed to deliver 3.6 metric tons of cargo to the lunar surface. A proposed larger ‚Äústretch tank‚Äù version would have enough lift to carry a human capsule with ascent capability, potentially returning astronauts to NASA‚Äôs <a href="https://www.nasa.gov/topics/moon-to-mars/lunar-outpost" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: var(--mc-link-color);font-weight: normal;text-decoration: underline;" target="_blank">Lunar Gateway</a>. Blue Moon uses a <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Davit" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: var(--mc-link-color);font-weight: normal;text-decoration: underline;" target="_blank">davit system</a> to lower payloads and rovers onto the surface. The BE-7 engine burns cryogenic hydrogen and oxygen (LH2/LOX), intentionally using a high performance fuel that can be made from water, thought to be frozen in shadowed craters on the moon‚Äôs poles, which are a likely landing target. The LH2 is also used in fuel cells for electric power. Bezos spent much of the presentation on a vision for future humans living in space, calling for the development of space infrastructure to enable the next generation of entrepreneurs. His group concluded that <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/O%27Neill_cylinder" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: var(--mc-link-color);font-weight: normal;text-decoration: underline;" target="_blank">O'Neill cylinder</a> space stations (<a href="https://space.nss.org/the-colonization-of-space-gerard-k-o-neill-physics-today-1974/" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: var(--mc-link-color);font-weight: normal;text-decoration: underline;" target="_blank">original 1974 paper</a>) are much better than planetary surfaces for human habitation due to: 1) being effectively unlimited in surface area, 2) being close to Earth to reduce communication delay, 3) having easier climate control, and 4) being able to exactly match Earth‚Äôs gravity through spin. This talk feels like Bezos positioning himself as a critic of Musk‚Äôs focus on Mars, but it seems like a conversation worth having.</p>

    <p style="line-height: 150%;margin: 10px 0;padding: 0;mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: var(--body-color);font-family: 'Lora', Georgia, 'Times New Roman', serif;font-size: 16px;text-align: left;"><strong>SpaceX is planning to launch 60 Starlink test satellites tomorrow</strong> (May 15th) on a Falcon 9 with a reused fairing. They‚Äôre <a href="https://twitter.com/elonmusk/status/1127388838362378241" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: var(--mc-link-color);font-weight: normal;text-decoration: underline;" target="_blank">packed in to the fairing like sardines</a> with a very different form factor than the <a href="https://space.skyrocket.de/doc_sdat/microsat-2.htm" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: var(--mc-link-color);font-weight: normal;text-decoration: underline;" target="_blank">first Starlink test hardware</a> launched a year ago. SpaceX plans to start launching units for the actual Internet service constellation later this year, which FCC applications suggest could be as large as 12,000 satellites. The satellites to be launched tomorrow <a href="https://spacenews.com/spacex-to-launch-dozens-of-starlink-satellites-may-15-more-starlink-launches-to-follow/" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: var(--mc-link-color);font-weight: normal;text-decoration: underline;" target="_blank">have working ground antennas and electric propulsion</a>, but lack the laser inter-satellite links that will be needed for the final network to have the desired bandwidth. Rumor is that the laser-interlinks have been expensive and hard to align (unsurprisingly, as this is mostly unproven technology, and continuously aligning a laser from one moving satellite to another in LEO is challenging to say the least), and so microwave interlinks may be used initially. Related: a detailed <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QEIUdMiColU" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: var(--mc-link-color);font-weight: normal;text-decoration: underline;" target="_blank">analysis video looking at the Starlink FCC proposal</a> and simulating how interconnections could work between orbital planes.</p>
